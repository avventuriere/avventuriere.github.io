I am a second year doctorate student in the Department of Linguistics and an Associate in the Neurobiology of Language program at the University of Connecticut. At UConn Linguistics my advisor is Susi Wurmbrand. At NBL my mentor is Paul Allopenna.

My research interests lie primarily in theoretical syntax. Currently I work on rightward extraposition, indexical shifting, and case and agreement. Check my Research page for some tidbits from current and past projects.

Main sources of data for my research are fieldwork and psycholinguistic experiments. As a fieldworker, I am involved with Tatar, Balkar, and Kyrghyz (Turkic), Buryat (Mongolic), Japanese (Japonic), Akebou (GTM; Kwa), and Nepali (Eastern Pahari) languages. As an amateur experimentalist, I am currently learning to use EEG and eye‑tracking under the supervision of Jon Sprouse and Gitte Joergensen respectively.
